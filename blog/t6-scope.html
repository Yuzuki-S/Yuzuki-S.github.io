Scope

What 'scope' is, and how it works in JavaScript.

Scope defines the accessibility of variables and there are two types of scope: Global scope and Local scope. Variables in the global scope are defined outside of a function and they can be referred throughout a program. Variables in the local scope are declared inside a function and they can be referred only inside the function. To explain them in daily life - an example is food in a flat. Everyone shares items such as oil, sugar, salt etc (in my flat) so they are in global scope as everyone can have access to them. On the other hand, in the local scope, all food in my drawer and in my pantry is mine where nobody else can have access to them.
